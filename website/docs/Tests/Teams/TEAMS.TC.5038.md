# TEAMS.TC.5038 - Private Meeting Scheduling Review

## Description

This informational test reviews the status of private meeting scheduling in Microsoft Teams.

## Policy Statement

Private meetings are not visible on users' calendars. This informational test reviews the status of private meeting scheduling to ensure it aligns with organizational meeting visibility requirements.

## Why This Matters

Private meeting scheduling allows users to create meetings that don't appear in other users' calendars or meeting lists, providing privacy for sensitive discussions.

## Why This Matters

- **Privacy:** Protects sensitive meeting topics
- **Confidentiality:** Keeps private discussions discreet
- **Governance:** Affects meeting visibility and oversight
- **Audit Trail:** Impacts meeting tracking capabilities
- **Compliance:** May affect recordkeeping requirements
- **Transparency:** Balances privacy vs. organizational visibility

## Privacy Considerations

### Benefits of Private Meetings:
- Sensitive topics remain confidential
- HR discussions protected
- Executive meetings maintain privacy
- Personal meetings stay discrete
- Reduces calendar clutter for others

### Governance Considerations:
- Private meetings harder to audit
- Less visibility for management oversight
- Compliance tracking more challenging
- Discovery for legal holds complicated
- Organizational transparency reduced

## Current Status Review

This test provides an informational review of whether private meeting scheduling is currently enabled or disabled. Organizations should evaluate this setting based on their privacy requirements and governance needs.

**Consider enabling if:**
- Privacy for sensitive meetings is required
- HR or confidential discussions are common
- Executive privacy is important
- Personal privacy valued

**Consider disabling if:**
- Full meeting transparency required
- Audit and compliance needs paramount
- Organizational visibility important
- Discovery requirements strict

## Configuration Steps

1. Navigate to [Teams Admin Center - Meeting Policies](https://admin.teams.microsoft.com/policies/meetings)
2. Select the appropriate policy
3. Under **General** section, find **Private meeting scheduling**
4. Toggle **On** or **Off** based on your requirements
5. Click **Save**

## Best Practices

If enabled:
- Communicate appropriate use guidelines
- Define what qualifies as "private"
- Maintain some visibility for compliance
- Implement training on proper usage
- Balance privacy with accountability

If disabled:
- Ensure alternative methods for sensitive meetings exist
- Communicate policy to users
- Provide guidance on appropriate meeting titles
- Consider selective enablement for specific roles
